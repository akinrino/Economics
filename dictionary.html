Economics

A social science geared towards the allocation of scarce resources.

Scarcity

In economics scarcity does not mean small or little. It arises when the demand for a resource is greater than the supply. As a result of scarcity economic agent has to make the best decision in allocating scarce resources.

Supply

This is the amount of resourse that a producer is willing and able to sell at a given point in time

Demand

This is the amount of goods and services that a consumer is willing and able to purchase at a given point in time

Equilibrium price

This is the price at which the quantity demanded and the quantity supplied is equal. In macroeconomics the aggregate demand and the aggregate supply in the economy is equal

Aggregate Demand

This is the total amount of goods and services that consumers are willing and able to buy at a given price level in the economy during a period of time

Aggregate Supply

This is the total amount of goods and services that producers are willing and able to sell at a given price level in the economy during a period of time

Inflation

An increase in the price level of goods and services in the economy at a given point in time

Deflation

A decrease in the price level of goods and services in the economy at a period of time

Disinflation

A decrease in the inflation rate. For example the inflation rate in March is 10% and in the next month, April the inflation rate is 8%.

Ceteris Paribus

This is a Latin phrase commonly used in economcs which means "All other things beings equal".

Elasticity

A measure of the response of an economic variable to a change in another economic variable

Gross Domestic Product

The amount of goods and services produced in a country at a given point in time

Law of Demand

All other things being equal, the higher the price the lower the quantity demanded and the lower the price the higher the quantity demanded

Law of Supply

All other things being equal, the lower the price the lower the quantity supplied and the higher the price the higher the quantity supplied

The invisible hand

Refers to the benefits society enjoys due to the selfish interest of individuals. This was propounded by Adam Smith to advocate for the benefits of Capitalism
